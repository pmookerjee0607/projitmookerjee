My Approach

How I think about the work

Beyond credentials and case studies — these are the convictions that shape how I lead, partner, and build.

01

Clarity is a leadership act

The best technical strategies fail when teams cannot articulate what they are doing or why. I treat clarity — in language, in priorities, in trade-offs — as one of the most underrated levers a leader has.

02

People before frameworks

SAFe, Scrum, Lean — these are tools, not religions. The right operating model is the one that fits the team, the market, and the moment. My job is to read the context and choose well, not to enforce orthodoxy.

03

Trust compounds quietly

I have seen multi-million-dollar deals close on the strength of relationships built three years prior. Showing up consistently, taking accountability when things go wrong, telling clients what they need to hear — these are not soft skills. They are commercial assets.

04

Curiosity outlasts expertise

The technologies I led with five years ago are not the ones I lead with today. Generative AI, agentic systems, responsible governance — staying useful means staying a student. I take learning seriously, and I take it personally.

The Human Side

Beyond the resume

Frameworks tell you how. Cultures tell you why. Leaders bridge the two — and the best ones know which side needs more attention on any given Tuesday.

— A working principle
01
Reads the room (and the silence)
Years across cultures taught me that what isn't said often matters more than what is. I notice hesitation, look for who has gone quiet in a meeting, and follow up one-on-one. Most "people problems" are actually unspoken signal problems.
→
02
Calm under escalation
When a launch slips or a client is unhappy, the team needs a leader who lowers the temperature, not raises it. I lead with diagnosis before defense, and I take the heat so my team can focus on the fix.
→
03
A genuine student of cultures
I read history, eat my way through new cities, and ask questions that go beyond small talk. Understanding where people come from — literally and figuratively — is how I build trust faster than tenure usually allows.
→
04
Writes well, on purpose
Clear writing is clear thinking. I have written investment cases, exec summaries, client emails, and team-wide updates that get read — because I care about the reader's time and the precision of the message.
→
05
Coaches, doesn't just manage
My favorite professional moments are not won deals — they are when someone I mentored gets promoted, leads their first major program, or pushes back on me with a better idea. Building leaders is the most lasting work I do.
→
06
Honest about trade-offs
I don't sell certainty I don't have. I tell clients what I would do if it were my budget, my team, my reputation. That candor has won more business than any pitch deck.
→
